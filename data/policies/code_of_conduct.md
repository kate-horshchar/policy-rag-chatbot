# Code of Conduct
**Sunshine Consulting**
*Effective Date: January 1, 2026 | Last Revised: March 30, 2026*
*Owner: People Operations Team*

---

## 1. Introduction

At Sunshine Consulting, we believe that a respectful and inclusive workplace is the foundation of good work. This code of conduct applies to all employees, contractors, and anyone representing Sunshine Consulting — in the office, on client sites, at events, or online.

---

## 2. How We Treat Each Other

We expect everyone at Sunshine Consulting to:

- **Be respectful** — treat colleagues, clients, and partners with kindness and professionalism
- **Be inclusive** — welcome different perspectives, backgrounds, and working styles
- **Be honest** — communicate openly and take responsibility for mistakes
- **Be supportive** — help teammates when they need it and celebrate each other's wins

We don't expect perfection, but we do expect good faith effort from everyone.

---

## 3. What We Don't Tolerate

The following behaviors are not acceptable at Sunshine Consulting:

- Harassment of any kind — verbal, physical, or written
- Discriminatory jokes or comments related to gender, race, religion, age, disability, sexual orientation, or nationality
- Bullying, intimidation, or creating a hostile work environment
- Sharing someone's personal information without their consent
- Retaliation against anyone who raises a concern in good faith

This applies to in-person interactions, Slack, email, and any other communication channels.

---

## 4. What to Do If Something Goes Wrong

If you experience or witness behavior that doesn't align with this code of conduct, you have a few options:

- **Talk to your manager** — if you feel comfortable doing so
- **Contact People Operations** — email people@sunshineconsulting.com or reach out on Slack #hr-help
- **Submit anonymously** — use the anonymous reporting form at the link pinned in #hr-help

There is no wrong way to raise a concern. All reports are taken seriously and treated with discretion.

---

## 5. What Happens After You Report

1. People Operations acknowledges your report within **2 business days**
2. An investigation is conducted — typically completed within **10 business days**
3. You are informed of the outcome to the extent possible given confidentiality
4. Appropriate action is taken, which may range from a conversation to termination depending on the situation

Anyone who reports a concern in good faith is protected from retaliation. This is non-negotiable.
