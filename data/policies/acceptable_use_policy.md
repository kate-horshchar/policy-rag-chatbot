# Acceptable Use Policy
**Sunshine Consulting**
*Effective Date: January 1, 2026 | Last Revised: March 30, 2026*
*Owner: IT Security Team*

---

## 1. Introduction

This policy is about using common sense when it comes to company systems, tools, and your online presence as a Sunshine Consulting employee. We trust our team to make good decisions — this document is here to set clear expectations, not to micromanage.

It applies to all employees, contractors, and anyone using Sunshine Consulting systems or networks.

---

## 2. Using Company Systems

Company laptops, accounts, and tools are primarily for work. A reasonable amount of personal use is fine — checking personal email, reading the news, or shopping during a lunch break is not a problem.

What we ask is that personal use:
- Doesn't interfere with your work or a colleague's work
- Doesn't involve anything illegal or inappropriate
- Doesn't put company data or systems at risk

---

## 3. What We Ask You Not to Do

A few things that genuinely cause problems for the company or your colleagues:

- **Torrenting or pirating content** on company networks or devices
- **Cryptocurrency mining** using company hardware or electricity
- **Installing unauthorized software** that hasn't been approved by IT
- **Accessing or sharing content** that is offensive, discriminatory, or inappropriate
- **Attempting to access systems or data** you are not authorized to use
- **Disabling security tools** like antivirus or the VPN

If you're unsure whether something is okay, ask IT or People Operations — we'd rather you check than guess.

---

## 4. Using AI Tools

We encourage the use of AI tools (such as ChatGPT, GitHub Copilot, Gemini, and others) to help with your work. They can be genuinely useful for drafting, coding, research, and brainstorming.

A few things to keep in mind:

- **Don't paste client data or confidential information** into public AI tools — this includes client names, contracts, financial data, or anything marked Confidential or Restricted
- **Review AI-generated output** before using it — AI can be wrong, and you are responsible for the work you submit
- **Approved AI tools for client work** must be confirmed with your manager and the client where applicable
- If you're not sure whether a tool is safe to use for a specific task, check with IT Security

---

## 5. Social Media

You're welcome to have a personal social media presence and mention that you work at Sunshine Consulting. A few guidelines:

- Don't share confidential company or client information publicly
- Don't make statements that could be mistaken for official Sunshine Consulting positions
- Be respectful when discussing the company, colleagues, or clients online
- If you're posting about a client project, get approval first

When in doubt, a good rule of thumb: would you be comfortable if your manager or the client saw this post?

---

## 6. Monitoring

Sunshine Consulting may monitor activity on company-owned systems and networks for security purposes. This is standard practice and helps us detect threats like malware or unauthorized access.

We are not in the business of reading your messages or tracking your every move — monitoring is focused on security, not surveillance. If a security incident occurs, logs may be reviewed as part of the investigation.

---

## 7. What Happens If Something Goes Wrong

If you accidentally violate this policy — for example, you clicked a phishing link or accidentally shared something you shouldn't have — please report it immediately to IT Security. Honest mistakes happen, and reporting quickly helps minimize the impact.

Intentional or repeated violations may result in loss of access to certain systems, disciplinary action, or in serious cases, termination.
