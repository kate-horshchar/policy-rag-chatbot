# Performance Review Policy
**Sunshine Consulting**
*Effective Date: January 1, 2026 | Last Revised: March 30, 2026*
*Owner: People Operations Team*

---

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy outlines the performance review process at Sunshine Consulting. Regular performance conversations help employees grow, align with company goals, and understand how their work is valued. This policy applies to all full-time employees who have completed their 90-day probationary period.

---

## 2. Review Cycle

Sunshine Consulting runs two formal check-ins per year:

| Review | Timing | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **Mid-Year Check-In** | June (Q2) | Progress update, goal adjustment, informal feedback |
| **Annual Review** | November–December (Q4) | Full performance evaluation, rating, merit increase |

- Managers are responsible for scheduling both conversations
- Employees complete a self-assessment in **Rippling** before each review
- All reviews and ratings are documented in Rippling under "Performance"

---

## 3. Rating Scale

Performance is assessed on a **1–5 scale**:

| Rating | Label | Description |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | Exceptional | Consistently exceeds expectations; significant impact beyond role |
| 4 | Strong | Frequently exceeds expectations; reliable high performer |
| 3 | Meets Expectations | Delivers on goals; performs well in the role |
| 2 | Needs Improvement | Partially meets expectations; improvement required in key areas |
| 1 | Unsatisfactory | Does not meet expectations; immediate action required |

A rating of **3 is a positive result** — it means the employee is doing their job well. Ratings of 4 and 5 reflect exceptional contribution.

---

## 4. Calibration

To ensure fairness and consistency across the company, People Operations runs a **calibration session** after managers submit ratings:

- Department heads and People Operations review all ratings together
- Calibration typically takes place in **November**
- Ratings may be adjusted during calibration to reflect company-wide standards
- Managers are informed of any changes before communicating results to employees

---

## 5. Merit Increases

Annual merit increases are tied to performance ratings and are effective **January 1** of the following year:

| Rating | Merit Increase Range |
|---|---|
| 5 – Exceptional | 6–8% |
| 4 – Strong | 4–6% |
| 3 – Meets Expectations | 2–4% |
| 2 – Needs Improvement | 0–1% |
| 1 – Unsatisfactory | 0% |

- Increases are subject to overall company budget and are not guaranteed
- Employees on an active PIP are not eligible for a merit increase
- Increases are communicated by managers during the annual review conversation

---

## 6. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

Employees who receive a rating of **2 or below** may be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).

- The PIP outlines specific areas for improvement, measurable goals, and a timeline (typically **60–90 days**)
- The manager and People Operations develop the PIP together
- Weekly check-ins are held during the PIP period
- Successful completion of a PIP results in the employee returning to regular performance management
- If performance does not improve, further action may be taken up to and including termination

Being placed on a PIP is not a punishment — it is a structured opportunity to get back on track with clear support and expectations.

---

## 7. How to Prepare for Your Review

**For employees:**
- Complete your self-assessment in Rippling at least **3 days before** your scheduled review meeting
- Reflect on your goals, achievements, and areas where you'd like to grow
- Come prepared with questions for your manager

**For managers:**
- Review the employee's self-assessment before the meeting
- Gather feedback from relevant colleagues or project leads if applicable
- Be specific — use concrete examples when discussing performance
